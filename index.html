Background

I am a Chicago native who travels often and loves to learn challenging things. I started my journey into software engineering intending to work remotely and travel year-round, but after delving deeper into the career possibilities in this field, I am now excited to build upon my current skills and learn with a team who values companionship and culture. My experience at National Geographic familiarized me with the inner workings of a large media company, and also challenged my ability to adapt to stressful situations and work through problems. From this experience, I realized the importance of cross-team collaboration and effective communication. Working in travel expanded my geographical knowledge as well as my attention to operational details, and I was able to learn about the many different moving parts that make such a large multi-faceted company work efficiently. What excites me most about starting a new career as a software engineer is knowing that I will be consistently challenged to learn new ways to handle problems. I’ll never feel stuck or bored in a position because I’m not learning anything new, and there will always be something to gain from pushing myself further.
